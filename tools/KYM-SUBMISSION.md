# Know Your Meme submission drafts — Dancing Banana attribution

Why this exists: KYM's *Peanut Butter Jelly Time* entry (the most-cited meme
history source) credits the 2002 Flash to Etrata & Flynn but names **no
creator for the banana itself** — an attribution vacuum that downstream
writers routinely compress into misattribution. No source disputes Trym's
creatorship (researched 4 Jul 2026: no rival claims anywhere; the deleted
Wikipedia "Dancing Banana" article also named no creator). Filling the KYM
vacuum is the single highest-leverage attribution move.

**How to submit (important):** under Trym's own name, openly as the creator —
KYM's culture rewards creator-confirmed origins (they interview creators) and
punishes stealth self-promo. Account as himself → edit-suggestion/comment on
the PBJT entry with draft 1 → new entry via "Add a Meme" with draft 2.
Disclose: "I'm the creator of the banana GIF — sources attached."

## Draft 1 — addition to the existing PBJT entry (Origin section)

> **The Dancing Banana emoticon**
>
> The dancing banana character featured in the animation predates the Flash
> by several years. According to its creator, Norwegian web developer Trym
> Stene, the animated GIF was drawn in 1999 in Animation Shop (bundled with
> Paint Shop Pro) and first posted to Norsk FreakForum, a Norwegian
> discussion board, where it was used as a celebration emoticon. By late 2001
> the banana had spread to numerous message boards as a standard `:banana:`
> smiley, before Etrata and Flynn paired it with the Buckwheat Boyz track in
> early 2002. Stene's account of the GIF's creation has been covered by
> City A.M.[1] and is documented on the Fandom meme wiki.[2]

## Draft 2 — standalone "Dancing Banana" entry

> **About**
>
> The Dancing Banana is an animated pixel-art GIF of a banana waving its arms
> in a two-phase dance, widely used as a forum emoticon under the code
> `:banana:` since the early 2000s. It is best known as the star of the 2002
> Flash animation *Peanut Butter Jelly Time*, though the character predates
> the Flash by roughly three years.
>
> **Origin**
>
> The GIF was created in 1999 by Norwegian web developer Trym Stene, who drew
> and animated the eight-frame banana in Animation Shop on a PC as a
> celebration emoticon for Norsk FreakForum, a Norwegian discussion
> board.[1][2] Stene has said he posted it whenever he finished something.
>
> **Spread**
>
> By late 2001 the banana was installed as a standard smiley on numerous
> vBulletin and phpBB forums. In early 2002, Ryan Gancenia Etrata and Kevin
> Flynn set the banana to the Buckwheat Boyz' "Peanut Butter Jelly Time" in a
> Flash animation posted to the Offtopic forums, which spread through
> Newgrounds, eBaum's World and YTMND. The animation reached mainstream
> television via NBC's *Ed* (November 2002) and *Family Guy*'s "The Courtship
> of Stewie's Father" (November 2005), in which Brian performs the song in a
> banana suit.[3] A now-deleted Wikipedia article documented the emoticon's
> popularity, including its adoption by the Amiga community, which produced a
> Boing Ball-themed variant. Stene runs a verified Giphy channel for the GIF
> and maintains the original files at trymstene.com.[4]
>
> **External References**
> [1] City A.M. — https://www.cityam.com/who-killed-the-gif-how-the-dancing-banana-got-peeled/
> [2] Fandom Meme Wiki — https://meme.fandom.com/wiki/Dancing_Banana
> [3] Family Guy Wiki — https://familyguy.fandom.com/wiki/Peanut_Butter_Jelly_Time
> [4] trymstene.com — https://trymstene.com/dancing-banana-gif-meme/
