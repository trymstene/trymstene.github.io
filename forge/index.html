Questions, answered

Is the Pixel Forge emoji maker free?

Yes — completely free, no account, no watermark. Draw in the browser and download transparent GIFs or PNGs ready for Discord, Twitch, Slack and Telegram.

Can I draw an animated GIF from scratch?

Yes — that is the whole idea. Most GIF makers only convert videos or stitch photos together; the Forge is a GIF drawing tool. You draw every frame in pixels, set the timing per frame, and export a real animated GIF.

Can I make animated Discord emojis with it?

Yes. Draw up to 16 frames with per-frame timing and export a transparent animated GIF at 128×128 pixels — exactly what Discord custom emojis require.

Can I make Twitch emotes with it?

Yes. Export at 112×112 pixels with a transparent background and Twitch auto-generates the 56px and 28px sizes when you upload.

Are the GIFs transparent?

Yes, every export supports transparency. Anything you leave unpainted stays see-through, so your emote floats over any chat background — no white box around it.

Can I import an existing GIF and remix it?

Yes. Import any GIF and it lands on the grid frame by frame, ready to redraw — including the original 1999 Dancing Banana, which is one click away.

Do I need an account or an app?

No. It runs entirely in your browser — nothing to install, no sign-up, no watermark. Finished pieces land on your Banana Pass so you can come back to them.

What is the Items Workshop?

The Forge’s second mode: draw a wearable item directly on the dancing banana — a hat, a sword, a rubber duck — and submit it to the banana club. Approved items become drops at the live rave: another visitor catches yours, wears it everywhere, and your name rides along as the maker.

Why pixel art for emotes?

Emotes render at 28–32 pixels in chat, where fine detail turns to mush. Pixel art drawn on a small grid stays crisp and readable — the same reason the 1999 Dancing Banana still works everywhere.
