How licensing works

Every use is quoted to the project.

There's no fixed price list, because a sticker pack and a national ad campaign aren't the same ask. Tell me what you're making and I'll quote it. Roughly, requests fall into three shapes:

Social & short campaign

One brand, a defined run, limited channels. The light end — usually a flat fee.

Merch & product

The banana on something you sell. Typically a fee plus a small royalty, so it scales with the product, not with guesswork.

Broadcast, film & games

Wide reach, long life, or exclusivity. Quoted per project — the serious end of the range.

Four things move a quote:

Use — where it appears Reach — how many people Term — how long Exclusivity — anyone else?

Non-exclusive and short is cheap; exclusive and perpetual is not.

Crypto, tokens & "bananacoin" projects

Possible — on stricter terms.

I don't endorse, promote, or post about token projects. The banana's image can be licensed for one, but the terms are deliberately different:

  • Image only — no use of my name, likeness, or any implied endorsement. Announcing the license itself as an association also needs written approval.
  • Fee paid in full, upfront, by bank transfer against an invoice — funds cleared before the license starts. No tokens, no equity, no "you'll be early", and no reversible payment methods (that includes PayPal).
  • A named counterparty — a legal entity, a jurisdiction, and a person who signs. I don't negotiate with anonymous teams or through "connectors".
  • Short term, non-exclusive, with a written right to terminate and require removal.
  • A morality clause — misuse ends the license immediately.
  • Everything in writing, through the form below. No calls, no video meets, nothing agreed verbally. If the deal needs a call to explain, it isn't ready.

Flag it honestly when you get in touch — it changes the quote, not your odds. Meet the terms above and this is a normal, friendly transaction.
